Who were the Signers

Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson
Widely regarded as the principal author of the Declaration of Independence. Jefferson wrote the initial draft, and his ideas on liberty, equality, and individual rights formed the foundation of the document.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence, Adams was part of the five-member committee responsible for drafting the Declaration. He provided significant input, particularly in terms of encouraging a break from Britain.
Benjamin Franklin
A member of the drafting committee who played a key role in editing and revising the draft. Franklin's diplomatic expertise and intellectual influence helped shape the tone and content of the Declaration.
Roger Sherman
A member of the drafting committee who contributed to the overall process. While his role in writing the document was less significant, his support was crucial.
Robert R. Livingston
Part of the drafting committee, Livingston contributed to the discussions but did not play a major role in writing the document. He later signed the Declaration.

While Jefferson is credited with writing the Declaration, it was a collaborative process, with significant input from Adams, Franklin, Sherman, and Livingston. These figures, along with others in the Continental Congress, helped shape the revolutionary document that proclaimed the American colonies' independence from Britain.
