Parks

Disneyland Resort, located in Anaheim, California, is home to two world-renowned theme parks:

  1. Disneyland Park
  2. Disney California Adventure Park

These two parks, each with its own distinct theme and attractions, provide an immersive experience for guests of all ages.

Both parks at Disneyland Resort are designed to offer something for everyone, blending nostalgia, innovation, and creativity into unforgettable experiences for all ages. Whether you're a fan of Disney classics or contemporary thrills, these two parks provide endless entertainment, immersive storytelling, and magical moments that bring joy to millions of guests every year.

Disneyland Park

Often referred to as Disneyland Resort’s “original” park, Disneyland Park opened its gates on July 17, 1955, under the vision of Walt Disney himself. It was the first-ever theme park built by The Walt Disney Company and remains a beloved destination for fans around the world. The park is divided into several themed lands, including:

  • Main Street U.S.A.
  • Adventureland
  • Fantasyland
  • Tomorrowland
  • Frontierland
  • Crittier Country
  • New Orleans Square

Disney California Adventure Park

Disneyland Park continues to evolve with new experiences, ensuring it remains fresh and exciting for visitors both new and returning.

Opening in 2001, Disney California Adventure Park offers a more contemporary experience inspired by the diverse culture and natural beauty of California. The park showcases California’s history, entertainment industry, and landmarks in a unique way. Key areas include:

  • Buena Vista Street
  • Grizzly Peak
  • Cars Land
  • Pixar Pier
  • Hollywood Land
  • Avengers Campus
